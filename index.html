Trump Proposes 25% Tariff on Foreign Cars Owned by Americans

By Alora Ignanco • April 18, 2025 • Washington, D.C.

President Donald Trump has announced a sweeping and highly controversial new trade measure: a 25% tariff on all foreign-manufactured vehicles imported during President Joe Biden’s term — and the kicker: the current owners will foot the bill.

“If you’re driving a foreign car that came in during the Biden years, you’re going to pay the price,” Trump said. “We’re done letting other countries take advantage of us — and that starts today.”

The tariff applies retroactively to any foreign vehicle imported between January 20, 2021 and January 20, 2025, regardless of ownership changes. Owners would pay based on the car’s original MSRP.

Consumer groups and legal experts are calling the move unprecedented. “It will face enormous constitutional and logistical challenges,” said Maya Raines, a legal analyst.

Dealerships and DMVs are overwhelmed as owners panic over compliance. Trump remains defiant: “These cars should never have been here. Now their owners can help rebuild American manufacturing.”

St. Louis Declares Toasted Ravioli a National Treasure

April 18, 2025 • St. Louis, MO

In a surprising bipartisan vote, city officials passed a resolution today recognizing toasted ravioli as a cultural artifact worthy of federal protection. Supporters say this is a crucial step toward preserving Midwestern identity.
